INTRODUCTION SECTION


Smart Cities Mission is an Indian government initiative launched in 2015 to develop 100 urban areas with advanced infrastructure and sustainable living. Its core mission focuses on creating inclusive, efficient cities through technology-driven solutions.


Objective

The mission aims to deliver core infrastructure like reliable water, electricity, sanitation, and mobility while ensuring a clean, sustainable environment. It promotes smart solutions for e-governance, safety, health, education, and affordable housing to enhance quality of life.


Development follows area-based models: retrofitting existing zones, redeveloping old areas, and greenfield projects for new expansions. Pan-city initiatives apply tech like intelligent traffic and IT networks across broader urban spaces.

FEATURES of SMART CITY-LUDHIANA

🏗️Core Infrastructure

Essential features include assured electricity and water supply, sanitation with solid waste management, and efficient public transport. Affordable housing for the poor and robust IT connectivity support digital services across cities.

📈Development Models

Features are implemented via area-based development (retrofitting, redevelopment, greenfield) and pan-city tech projects like bicycle sharing in Chandigarh. These ensure mixed land use, citizen participation, and resilient urban mobility.

♻️ Sustainability Features

Smart cities prioritize green energy with solar panels, smart grids, and renewable sources to cut emissions. Waste-to-energy plants and advanced recycling systems manage solid waste effectively, reducing landfill dependency. Water conservation uses smart meters and rainwater harvesting for efficient supply

🤝Citizen-Centric Tech

Digital platforms enable participatory governance, like apps for reporting issues and crowdsourced feedback. Smart healthcare includes telemedicine kiosks and real-time health monitoring in public spaces. Education hubs leverage e-learning centers with high-speed internet for inclusive access.

📊Data and Governance

Centralized command centers use IoT sensors and AI for real-time monitoring of traffic, utilities, and security. Open data portals empower citizens with transparent access to urban metrics, fostering informed participation.

💰Economic Hubs

Incubators and co-working spaces nurture startups, while digital markets boost local commerce. Public Wi-Fi hotspots and 5G networks drive e-commerce and remote work opportunities.
